Coffee is an agricultural product. Where it grows—and **what is in the soil**—shows up in the cup more often than people expect.

## Minerals and maturation

Volcanic regions tend to combine **mineral-rich soils** with **sharp day–night temperature swings** at elevation. That combination can slow cherry maturation and help develop complexity in the bean.

## From region to roast

Our roasting style is built to respect each origin’s character: we aim for clarity in lighter profiles and depth without harshness in darker ones. Explore the [origins](/origins/costa-rica) section to read about each coffee’s story and tasting notes.

## Try it yourself

Brew the same coffee two ways—see our [grind options](/brewing/grind-options) and [roasting options](/brewing/roasting-options) pages—then notice how grind and ratio shift sweetness and body. Small experiments teach faster than any tasting wheel.
